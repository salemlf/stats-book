# 6.4: The Method of Transformations

:::info[Summary of the Transformation Method]

Let $U = h(Y)$, where $h(y)$ is either an increasing or decreasing function of $y$ for all $y$ such that $f_Y(y) > 0$.

1. Find the inverse function, $y = h^{-1}(u)$.

2. Evaluate $\frac{dh^{-1}}{du} = \frac{d[h^{-1}(u)]}{du}$.

3. Find $f_U(u)$ by
   $$
   f_U(u) = f_Y[h^{-1}(u)] \left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du} \right|.
   $$

:::

Example 6.8 is a nice, worked out example of how to use the method of transformations in a multivariate situation.

:::tip[Example 6.8]

Let $Y_1$ and $Y_2$ have a joint density function given by

$$
f(y_1, y_2) =
\begin{cases}
    e^{-(y_1 + y_2)}, & 0 \leq y_1, 0 \leq y_2,\\
    0, & \text{elsewhere.}
\end{cases}
$$

Find the density function for $U = Y_1 + Y_2$.

<details>
    <summary>Solution</summary>
    <div>
        This problem must be solved in two stages: First, we will find the joint density of $Y_1$ and $U$; second, we will find the marginal density of $U$.
        The approach is to let $Y_1$ be fixed at a value $y_1 \geq 0$.
        Then $U = y_1 + Y_2$, and we can consider the one-dimensional transformation problem in which $U = h(Y_2) = y_1 + Y_2$.
        Letting $g(y_1, u)$ denote the joint density of $Y_1$ and $U$, we have, with $y_2 = u - y_1 = h^{-1}(u)$,

        $$
        g(y_1, u) =
        \begin{cases}
            f[y_1, h^{-1}(u)] \left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du} \right| = e^{-(y_1 + u - y_1)} (1), & 0 \leq y_1, 0 \leq u - y_1,\\
            0, & \text{elsewhere.}
        \end{cases}
        $$

        Simplifying, we obtain

        $$
        g(y_1, u) =
        \begin{cases}
            e^{-u}, & 0 \leq y_1 \leq u,\\
            0, & \text{elsewhere.}
        \end{cases}
        $$

        (Notice that $Y_1 \leq U$.) The marginal density of $U$ is then given by

        $$
        \begin{align*}
            f_U(u) & = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} g(y_1, u) dy_1\\
            & =
            \begin{cases}
                \int_{0}^{u} e^{-u} dy_1 = ue^{-u}, & 0 \leq u,\\
                0, & \text{elsewhere.}
            \end{cases}
        \end{align*}
        $$
    </div>

</details>

:::
