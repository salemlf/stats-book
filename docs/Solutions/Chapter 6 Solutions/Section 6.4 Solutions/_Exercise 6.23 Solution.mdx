a) We have $U_1 = h(Y) = 2Y - 1$, and $h(y)$ is an increasing function over the support of $f_Y$, $0 \leq y \leq 1$, so this implies $Y = \frac{U_1 + 1}{2} = h^{-1}(U_1)$.
Then $\left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du_1} \right| = \left|\frac{d}{du_1} \left(\frac{u_1 + 1}{2} \right) \right| = \frac{1}{2}$.
Also, $0 \leq \frac{u_1 + 1}{2} \leq 1 \Longleftrightarrow 0 \leq u_1 + 1 \leq 2 \Longleftrightarrow -1 \leq u_1 \leq 1$.
Thus,

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_{U_1}(u_1) & = f_Y[h^{-1}(u_1)] \left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du_1} \right|\\
    & = \left[
    \begin{cases}
        2 \left(1 - \left(\frac{u_1 + 1}{2} \right) \right), & -1 \leq u_1 \leq 1,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere}
    \end{cases} \right] \cdot \frac{1}{2}\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{1 - u_1}{2}, & -1 \leq u \leq 1,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$

b) We have $U_2 = h(Y) = 1 - 2Y$, and $h(y)$ is a decreasing function over $0 \leq y \leq 1$, which implies $Y = \frac{1 - U_2}{2} = h^{-1}(U_2)$.
Then $\left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du_2} \right| = \left|\frac{d}{du_2} \left(\frac{1 - u_2}{2} \right) \right| = \frac{1}{2}$.
Also, $0 \leq \frac{1 - u_2}{2} \leq 1 \Longleftrightarrow 0 \leq 1 - u_2 \leq 2 \Longleftrightarrow -1 \leq -u_2 \leq 1 \Longleftrightarrow 1 \geq u_2 \geq -1$.
Thus,

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_{U_2}(u_2) & = f_Y[h^{-1}(u_2)] \left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du_2} \right|\\
    & = \left[
    \begin{cases}
        2 \left(1 - \left(\frac{1 - u_2}{2} \right) \right), & -1 \leq u_2 \leq 1,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere}
    \end{cases} \right] \cdot \frac{1}{2}\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{u_1 + 1}{2}, & -1 \leq u_2 \leq 1,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$

c) We have $U_3 = h(Y) = Y^2$, and $h(y)$ is an increasing function over $0 \leq y \leq 1$, which implies $Y = \sqrt{U_3} = h^{-1}(U_3)$.
Then $\left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du_3} \right| = \left|\frac{d}{du_3} \left(u_3^{1/2} \right) \right| = \frac{1}{2} u_3^{-1/2}$.
Also, $0 \leq \sqrt{u_3} \leq 1 \Longleftrightarrow 0 \leq u_3 \leq 1$.
Thus,

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_{U_3}(u_3) & = f_Y[h^{-1}(u_3)] \left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du_3} \right|\\
    & = \left[
    \begin{cases}
        2 \left(1 - u_3^{1/2} \right), & 0 \leq u_3 \leq 1,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere}
    \end{cases} \right] \cdot \frac{1}{2} u_3^{-1/2}\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        u_3^{-1/2} - 1, & 0 \leq u_3 \leq 1,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$
