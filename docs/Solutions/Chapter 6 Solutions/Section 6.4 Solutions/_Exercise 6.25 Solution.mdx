Recall that

$$
f_{Y_1, Y_2}(y_1, y_2) =
\begin{cases}
    e^{-(y_1 + y_2)}, & y_1 > 0, y_2 > 0,\\
    0, & \text{elsewhere.}
\end{cases}
$$

Let $U = \frac{Y_1 + Y_2}{2}$ and fix $Y_1$ to a value $y_1 > 0$ so that $U = \frac{y_1 + Y_2}{2} = h(Y_2)$ and $Y_2 = 2U - y_1 = h^{-1}(U)$.
Then the joint density of $Y_1$ and $U$ will be

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_{Y_1, U}(y_1, u) & = f_{Y_1, Y_2}(y_1, h^{-1}(u)) \left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du} \right|\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        e^{-(y_1 + (2u - y_1))} (2), & y_1 > 0, 2u - y_1 > 0\\
        = 2e^{-2u}, & 0 < y_1 < 2u,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$

Then the density function for $U$, which is the marginal density of $U$, is

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_U(u) & = \int_{y_1 = -\infty}^{y_1 = \infty} f_{Y_1, U}(y_1, u) dy_1\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        \int_{y_1 = 0}^{y_1 = 2u} 2e^{-2u} dy_1\\
        = 2e^{-2u} y_1 \biggr|_{y_1 = 0}^{y_1 = 2u}\\
        = 2e^{-2u} (2u) - 2e^{-2u} (0)\\
        = 4ue^{-2u}, & 0 < 2u \Longleftrightarrow 0 < u,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$
