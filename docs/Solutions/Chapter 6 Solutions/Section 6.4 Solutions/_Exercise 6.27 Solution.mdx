a) Letting $W = Y^{1 / 2} = h(Y)$, we have $Y = W^2 = h^{-1}(W)$, and since $y > 0$ is the support of $Y$, we will have $\sqrt{y} = w > 0$ as the support of $W$.
Additionally, $\left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{dw} \right| = 2w$.
Thus,

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_W(w) & = f_Y(h^{-1}(w)) \left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{dw} \right|\\
    & = \left[
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{1}{\beta} e^{-(w^2) / \beta}, & w > 0,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere}
    \end{cases} \right] \cdot 2w\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{1}{\beta} 2w^{2 - 1} e^{-w^2 / \beta}, & w > 0,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$

This is exactly a Weibull density with $\alpha = \beta$ and $m = 2$.
