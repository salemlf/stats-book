a) Letting $U = h(Y) = Y^m$, then $Y = U^{1/m} = h^{-1}(U)$, and $u^{1/m} > 0 \Longleftrightarrow u > 0$.
So $\left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du} \right| = \left|\frac{1}{m} u^{\frac{1}{m} - 1} \right| = \frac{1}{m} u^{\frac{1}{m} - 1}$.
Thus,

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_U(u) & = f_Y(h^{-1}(u)) \left|\frac{dh^{-1}}{du} \right|\\
    & = \left[
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{1}{\alpha} m \left(u^{1/m} \right)^{m - 1} e^{-(u^{1/m})^m / \alpha}, & u > 0,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere}
    \end{cases} \right] \cdot \frac{1}{m} u^{\frac{1}{m} - 1}\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{1}{\alpha} e^{-u / \alpha}, & u > 0,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$

This is the density for an exponential distribution with mean $\alpha$.

b)

$$
\begin{align*}
    E(Y^k) & = \int_{y = 0}^{y = \infty} y^k \cdot \frac{1}{\alpha} my^{m - 1} e^{-y^m / \alpha} dy\\
    & = \int_{u = 0}^{u = \infty} \frac{1}{\alpha} u^{k/m} e^{-u / \alpha} du \quad \left(\text{letting $u = y^m$ so that $du = my^{m - 1} dy$} \right)\\
    & = \alpha^{\frac{k}{m}} \Gamma \left(\frac{k}{m} + 1 \right) \int_{u = 0}^{u = \infty} \frac{u^{(\frac{k}{m} + 1) - 1} e^{-u / \alpha}}{\alpha^{\frac{k}{m} + 1} \Gamma(\frac{k}{m} + 1)} du \quad \left(\text{the integrand here is a gamma density} \right)\\
    & = \alpha^{\frac{k}{m}} \Gamma \left(\frac{k}{m} + 1 \right) \cdot 1 = \alpha^{\frac{k}{m}} \Gamma \left(\frac{k}{m} + 1 \right)
\end{align*}
$$
